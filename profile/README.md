## 📖 Overview

<a href="#plasticity">Plasticity</a> is a professional NURBS-based CAD modeling application designed for product designers, concept artists, and industrial designers who need precise solid geometry creation without the complexity and cost of traditional enterprise CAD tools.

The <a href="#plasticity">NURBS solid modeling foundation</a> creates mathematically precise geometry that maintains accuracy regardless of scale — not polygon meshes with visible faceting at close range, but smooth continuous surfaces that represent the exact geometry defined by the modeling operations. This precision is essential for product design and industrial design where manufacturing tolerances, surface quality, and geometric accuracy matter fundamentally. NURBS geometry behaves differently from polygon modeling: boolean operations produce clean intersections, fillet and chamfer operations produce industry-standard edge treatments, and the resulting geometry exports directly to manufacturing workflows without mesh conversion issues. The <a href="#plasticity">keyboard-driven modeling workflow</a> uses a fast keyboard shortcut system derived from the industrial CAD paradigm — every modeling operation accessible through keyboard commands for experienced CAD modelers who want efficiency comparable to professional CAD tools while maintaining a more approachable interface than traditional enterprise software.

The <a href="#plasticity">boolean operation quality</a> handles union, subtract, and intersect operations between solid bodies with the clean intersection geometry that professional product design requires — boolean operations that produce well-formed solid bodies suitable for further modeling rather than the messy intersection geometry that polygon boolean operations often create. The <a href="#plasticity">fillet and chamfer operations</a> create the edge treatments that define manufactured product aesthetics — variable-radius fillets, chamfers at precise angles, blend surfaces between non-parallel faces — the surface quality vocabulary of industrial design that distinguishes Plasticity from mesh-based modeling tools.

<a href="#plasticity">Sketching and curve tools</a> create precise 2D profiles for 3D modeling operations — drawing curves, circles, and complex profiles as the foundation for extrude, revolve, loft, and sweep operations that generate solid geometry from 2D inputs. <a href="#plasticity">Shell and offset operations</a> create thin-shell forms from solid bodies — the wall-thickness modeling operation that converts solid forms into hollow shells for consumer product design, enclosure design, and any application where material thickness matters.

<a href="#plasticity">Blender bridge integration</a> exports Plasticity geometry directly to Blender for rendering, animation, and further artistic work — combining Plasticity's precise CAD modeling with Blender's rendering capabilities for product visualization workflows. <a href="#plasticity">STEP and IGES export</a> produces industry-standard CAD interchange formats that flow directly into manufacturing toolchains, engineering analysis software, and downstream CAD tools.

## 🤔 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What makes Plasticity different from Blender for 3D modeling?</summary>

Plasticity uses NURBS solid modeling for mathematically precise geometry with clean boolean operations and precise edge treatments. Blender uses polygon meshes that are more flexible for organic forms but less precise for hard-surface product design.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What file formats does Plasticity export?</summary>

OBJ for general 3D use, STEP and IGES for CAD interchange with manufacturing tools, and through the Blender bridge for direct Blender integration. Check current Plasticity documentation for the complete export format list.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Plasticity parametric or direct modeling?</summary>

Plasticity uses direct modeling — manipulating geometry through face and edge selection rather than a history tree of parametric operations. This makes it faster and more flexible for concept design.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Plasticity run on Apple Silicon?</summary>

Yes. Current versions of Plasticity support Apple Silicon Macs natively.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the Blender bridge?</summary>

The Blender bridge is a Plasticity plugin that exports geometry directly to Blender, enabling a combined Plasticity CAD modeling and Blender rendering workflow.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Plasticity suitable for engineering and manufacturing?</summary>

Plasticity produces STEP export suitable for manufacturing toolchains. For full parametric engineering workflows with assembly management and design history, traditional CAD tools may be more appropriate.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the pricing for Plasticity?</summary>

Plasticity offers Indie Studio and Enterprise pricing tiers. Check the Plasticity website for current pricing.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Plasticity handle organic modeling?</summary>

Plasticity is optimized for hard-surface and geometric forms. Other tools like Blender with Sculpt mode are typically better suited for organic forms.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What tutorials are available for Plasticity?</summary>

Official tutorials, YouTube content from the community, and workflow guides for common design tasks are available. Check the Plasticity website and YouTube for current tutorial resources.

</details>
